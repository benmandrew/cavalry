Why the spread is @@RANGE@@

  • Arithmetic & control flow (@@SP_ARITH@@). On loops of scalar math — sum_loop, collatz, primes_trial — the interpreter's cost is per-operation dispatch: walking the abstract syntax tree (AST), and threading a persistent variable map where the compiler emits a mutable ref. A steady order-of-magnitude, workload-independent.
  • Recursion (@@SP_RECUR@@×). ackermann makes millions of calls. Each interpreted call rebuilds a local environment and does a hashtable lookup for the procedure; the compiled binary makes a real OCaml call. Call overhead compounds.
  • Arrays (@@SP_ARRAY@@×). Both the interpreter and the compiler mutate arrays in place, so a[i] := v is an O(1) store either way. What is left is per-element interpreter overhead — bounds handling and the index/value AST walk — a constant factor on top of the scalar gap, not the algorithmic blow-up an earlier copy-on-write store made it.
  • Native vs Zarith (@@NZ_RANGE@@). The cav compile default is arbitrary-precision Zarith, matching the verified unbounded-integer model. --native-int compiles to wrapping 63-bit ints and is sound only because its verification gate additionally proves no overflow — worth an extra ~1.3× on arithmetic, ~2× on the call-bound Ackermann.

All three variants were checked to produce identical output before timing. Reproduce with bash bench/run.sh then python3 bench/analyze.py; programs are generated by bench/gen.py.
